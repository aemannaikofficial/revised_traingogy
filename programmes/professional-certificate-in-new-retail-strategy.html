PROGRAMME

AIMS The Professional Certificate has the following aims: To provide a flexible and credit accumulation pathway to enable adult learners to upgrade their skills and knowledge in the workplace. To provide adult learners with recognized certificated learning which contributes to their career advancement.

LEARNING OUTCOMES On completion of the Professional Certificate in New Retail Strategy learners will be able to: Gain more comprehensive body of knowledge to develop, formulate and operationalise new retail strategies and operations that are suitable for their business environment Develop a range of professional work application skills including techniques and attributes essential for effective and successful new retail performance.

PROGRAMME

STRUCTURE The Professional Certificate comprises a cluster of three specific modules each of which must be completed. Each of the following numbered modules carry 10 credits under the Qualifications and Credit Framework at Level 4. The three modules are: Overview of New Retailing Competitiveness and Retail Metrics for Growth Peak Performance Blueprint: People Performance.

ASSESSMENT

STRUCTURE Each Module has a 50 Multiple Choice Question final examination which must be completed within 1.5 hours. NOTIONAL HOURS FOR EACH MODULE The normal prescribed notional hours are 100 notional hours for each module. The notional learning time encompasses all time (formal classes consisting of both face-to-face or online live sessions, self-study, reading, researching the internet for information, final examination preparation) spent by the learner in pursuit of achieving the intended learning outcomes.

​Note: Those learners with prior knowledge and experience related to the module contents will require less than the 100 notional hours prescribed. ​NOTIONAL HOURS FOR FULL PROGRAMME A total of 300 hours is prescribed for the 30 credits Professional Certificate.

​The notional learning time encompasses all time (formal classes, self-study, reading, searching the internet for information, final examination preparation) spent by the learner in pursuit of achieving the intended learning outcomes. ENTRY REQUIREMENTS This is an open entry programme wherein no prior knowledge is required. Applicants meeting any one or more of the following are eligible to undertake the programme: Applicants should normally be at least 16 years old or Possess a Level 3 (QCF/RQF) Certificate or Possess GCSEs at grade C or above, or equivalents or Possess working Experience PROGRAMME DELIVERY AND DURATION Participants will be required to successfully complete the following 3 modules each of 10 credits: Overview of New Retailing Competitiveness and Retail Metrics for Growth Peak Performance Blueprint: People Performance The programme will be delivered via Blended Learning wherein each module will have 16 hours of face-to-face sessions and students will engage in further online learning and interaction with online tutors via a designated online learning portal.

Each module duration is 1 month followed by an online final examination consisting of 50 MCQs. The programme can be completed in 3 months including undertaking the required online final examinations. MODULE SPECIFICATION Module 1: : Overview of New Retailing Learning Outcomes Describe the evolution of the retailing industry landscape and its implications for modern retailing practices to sustain business continuity and competitiveness Explain internal and external environmental scanning to facilitate new retail seamless strategy formulation.

Identify and apply key principles, techniques and tools required to successfully formulate and implement new retail seamless strategy for a product or service including evaluating business models options. Examine key concepts and techniques to undertake both offline and online retailing activities.
